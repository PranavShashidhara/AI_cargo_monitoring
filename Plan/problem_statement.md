Cargo Monitoring
A global pharmaceutical distributor partners with OnAsset to transport temperature-sensitive vaccines and specialty medicines across multiple countries using sensor-equipped smart containers. Each shipment streams real-time telemetry (temperature, humidity, shock, geolocation, customs status, flight schedules, etc.) to a central platform. Delays caused by weather, port congestion, customs holds, or equipment failures can compromise product integrity and disrupt tightly coordinated vaccination schedules at hospitals and public health clinics. The company wants to deploy AI monitor agents that continuously detect anomalies, predict risk of spoilage or delay, and autonomously recommend route adjustments, alternative carriers, cold-storage interventions, or priority customs escalation—while remaining compliant with GDP/FDA regulations and maintaining a clear human-in-the-loop approval process.
The business challenge is to design an agentic AI system capable of triggering cascading operational actions when shipment risks are detected. For example, if a delay threatens vaccine viability, the system should not only suggest rerouting but also coordinate downstream effects: notify healthcare providers, recommend rescheduling patient appointments, update inventory forecasts, initiate insurance claims documentation, and ensure audit-ready compliance logs. The solution must balance automation with regulatory constraints, explainability, and risk management, minimizing financial loss and public health disruption while ensuring traceability, accountability, and ethical oversight.
